I am currently a final-year Ph.D. candidate at the University of Chinese Academy of Sciences (UCAS), where I am fortunate to be advised by Prof. Tiande Guo and Prof. Congying Han.

At present, I am a visiting Ph.D. Student at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign (UIUC), where I am fortunate to be advised by Prof. Nan Jiang.

Prior to my graduate studies, I received my Bachelor's degree in Mathematics from the University of Science and Technology of China (USTC).

Research Interests

My research interests focus on trustworthy reinforcement learning, spanning theory, algorithms, and applications. I am dedicated to developing theoretically grounded algorithms that can effectively guide practical scenarios, aiming to bridge the gap between theoretical guarantees and empirical performance.

Broadly, I am also interested in the interplay between machine learning and optimization, with a particular focus on policy optimization, machine learning for combinatorial optimization (ML4CO), training of large language models (LLMs) and vision-language models (VLMs).
